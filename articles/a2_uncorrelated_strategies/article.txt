“Building a Simple Momentum-Based Reallocation Model to Try and Beat the Market”

Market conditions vary as a result of a nearly infinite number of factors. Sometimes, static portfolios work well, and other times more complex models can outperform.
By matching portfolio strategy to market conditions, greater returns may be achieved.

A simple target factor could be how sensitive the market is to momentum.
Given certain market sentiment, economic conditions, political or legal conditions, financial markets may show a tendency to either follow collective trends, or may ignore trends and remain stable.
By defining a simple momentum indicator, and a model to rebalance a portfolio based on that momentum indicator, it may be possible to enjoy the benefits of market stability, whilst also taking advantage of increased market activity during momentum trends.

This investigation will consider three uncorrelated assets: SPY, GLD, and IEF. This should give a good representation of uncorrelated assets with which to rebalance a portfolio to achieve portfolio diversity.

Building the Simple Momentum Model

My model will leverage this simple principle:
If the daily price of an asset is above the 100 day moving average for that price, then allocate to that asset. Otherwise, do not.
In addition, I will add a reallocation limit, where some portion of each assert cannot be sold, to retain some level of diversification during a trend following phase.
In theory, this strategy will try to follow trends in each security, dynamically riding rallies as they appear.
